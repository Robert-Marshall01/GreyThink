## Overview

This curriculum integrates Grey Distributed concepts into education at every level, from elementary awareness through doctoral research. The goal is to ensure that distributed infrastructure becomes as fundamental to human understanding as mathematics, language, and civics.

---

## Part I: Curriculum Philosophy

### Educational Objectives

```yaml
curriculum_objectives:
  primary:
    - Ensure universal distributed systems literacy
    - Transmit Grey Distributed principles across generations
    - Develop future stewards and contributors
    - Embed infrastructure awareness in citizenship
    
  secondary:
    - Foster innovation in distributed systems
    - Create academic research partnerships
    - Build global educational community
    - Support career pathways in infrastructure
```

### Pedagogical Approach

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Progressive Complexity** | Age-appropriate concepts building on prior knowledge |
| **Hands-on Learning** | Practical exercises with real systems |
| **Global Perspective** | Multi-cultural and international context |
| **Ethical Integration** | Ethics woven throughout, not separate |
| **Research Connection** | Connection to current research frontiers |

## Part II: K-12 Curriculum

### Elementary School (Ages 5-10)

#### Learning Objectives

Students will understand:
- What infrastructure means and why it matters
- How computers talk to each other
- Why sharing and cooperation work better than isolation
- Basic concepts of fairness in sharing

#### Curriculum Modules

```yaml
elementary_modules:
  kindergarten_1st_grade:
    unit_1:
      title: "The Helpers Around Us"
      concepts:
        - Infrastructure in daily life
        - Roads, water, electricity
        - Invisible helpers (internet)
      activities:
        - Draw infrastructure you use
        - Scavenger hunt for infrastructure
        - Building block city with connections
        
    unit_2:
      title: "Sharing is Caring"
      concepts:
        - Why sharing works
        - Taking turns
        - Fairness in groups
      activities:
        - Sharing games
        - Resource distribution exercise
        - Creating fair rules together
        
  2nd_3rd_grade:
    unit_1:
      title: "Messages Across the World"
      concepts:
        - How messages travel
        - Networks of friends
        - Reliability (what if one friend is absent?)
      activities:
        - Human telephone network game
        - Message relay with failures
        - Drawing network diagrams
        
    unit_2:
      title: "The Internet is Many Computers"
      concepts:
        - Data centers and servers
        - Geographic distribution
        - Why redundancy matters
      activities:
        - Virtual data center tour
        - Mapping internet locations
        - Backup plan exercises
        
  4th_5th_grade:
    unit_1:
      title: "Introduction to Distributed Systems"
      concepts:
        - Centralized vs. distributed
        - Fault tolerance basics
        - Consensus (agreeing on facts)
      activities:
        - Distributed voting simulation
        - Failure scenario exercises
        - Design a reliable system
        
    unit_2:
      title: "Digital Citizenship"
      concepts:
        - Responsibility in digital spaces
        - Privacy and security basics
        - Being part of a digital community
      activities:
        - Privacy audit exercise
        - Community guideline creation
        - Digital helper role-play
```

### High School (Ages 14-18)

#### Learning Objectives

Students will:
- Understand distributed systems technically and conceptually
- Analyze trade-offs and make reasoned decisions
- Apply ethical frameworks to real problems
- Prepare for careers or further study

#### Course Offerings

